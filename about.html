weronika gajda | about


architect and interdisciplinary PhD researcher at KULeuven

After working in architecture firm in Poland my interests shifted towards digital technology as a biased tool creating our environment and increasing part of the surrounding. I obtained a master degree in architecture from KULeuven in Gent in Belgium. During my studies I started questioning dependencies between ubiquitous presence of digital environments, their representations and interaction with architecture and space. Reflecting on the impact of media ecology on space and reconstructing public sphere from the protest in Poland with data collected from participants resulted in the project called Citography. It revealed relationships of public space and sphere trough social media platforms. To continue this work I started an interdisciplinary PhD research in Architecture and Technology at KULeuven in Gent. Now, I focus on development of digital tools that could become a part of inclusive public spaces and play and an active part of collectively created urban structure.
