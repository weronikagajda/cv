weronika gajda | about


architect and interdisciplinary PhD researcher at KULeuven

Weronika Gajda obtained a Bachelor’s degree in Architecture and Urban Planning from Warsaw University of Technology, Poland in 2017. After graduating, she worked for 2 years as an architect in medusagroup architects in Poland. In 2022, she obtained a Masters in Architecture from KU Leuven, where she stayed on for the interdisciplinary PhD research, under the title “Citography: Mapping Collective Experiences in Public Spaces” in the departments of Architecture and Engineering Technologies. Her main research interests are at the intersection of public space, media ecologies and collectiveness. Central to her work is the question of how digital technologies impact inhabiting and designing the cities.
